Turkey’s Fight for Women’s Rights

Women In Turkey

Gender inequality in Turkey remains a significant issue despite legal reforms aimed at promoting gender equality. Women face unequal access to justice, political participation, work, and education. Traditional gender roles and cultural norms still restrict women's possibilities, particularly in rural areas. One of the main concerns is violence against women, which includes femicide and domestic abuse. Even though action and awareness have increased, there are still constraints to implementing legislation and bringing forth social change.

Issues Turkish Women Are Facing

In Turkey, women are facing a number of related problems, including high rates of femicide and gender-based violence. Issues like low labor force participation and wage gaps are worsened by a lack of childcare and unstructured work. Women in Turkey are struggling with a lack of representation in politics and decision-making. Educational barriers and pressure for early marriage still persist in some areas. Limited access to safe abortion despite legal allowances and widespread online harassment are other challenges Turkish women continue to encounter.

UN Sustainability Goal For Turkish Women

The United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 5: Achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls is the one that Turkish women are most directly concerned with. Turkey's achievement of this objective depends on various areas from implementing policies and services that prevent femicide and gender-based violence, reducing economic disparities by increasing women's employment and providing equal compensation, ensuring quality childcare and reproductive health care, and increasing the representation of women in parliament and corporate boards through regulations and leadership programs.

Legal Reforms To Safeguard Turkish Women

The majority of the legal laws required to safeguard women are already in the hands of Turkish politicians. Despite introducing the necessary laws to support women, Law No. 6284 has presented problems with execution as effective risk evaluations and surveillance are lacking, and courts have the authority to impose orders for brief periods. The facilities like the Ministry of Family and Social Services' Violence Prevention and Monitoring Centers were set up to monitor protective orders, improve coordination between the police, courts, and social services, and offer services like risk assessment, shelter placement, and ongoing victim assistance.

Organizational Approaches For Womens Rights In Turkey

Women's NGOs have become increasingly influential in gender politics in Turkey. Turkish women’s rights work is driven mostly by civil society organizations that combine campaigning, direct assistance, and data‑based advocacy. While WWHR–New Ways combines basic human rights education with legislative influencing and international network-building, established NGOs like the Mor Çatı Women’s Shelter Foundation run shelters, legal clinics, and nationwide training for social workers and lawyers.
